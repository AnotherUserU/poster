1) Introduction

Customer churn is an important issue in the telecom industry because losing customers affects revenue and retention costs. This project aims to identify customers who are likely to churn so companies can respond earlier and take proactive actions to improve retention.

4) Feature Engineering

  • No major null-value issue was found.
  • "Total Charges" was converted from object to numeric and missing values were filled with 0.
  • One-hot encoding was used for non-ordered categorical features.
  • Ordinal encoding was used for "Contract" and the binary label "Churn".

7) Conclusion

Random Forest can be used effectively for customer churn prediction. Feature engineering, SMOTE, threshold tuning, and model optimization improved the model's ability to detect churn. The final model also provides useful business insight for customer retention decisions.
